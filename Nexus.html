Meaning Operating System

A meaning system for humans and machines

Orient is where knowledge becomes shared understanding — captured, resolved, framed, measured and remembered — so people and the agents working alongside them act from the same picture of what's true, decided and ready to move forward.

The shift

Two customers of meaning. Humans use it to think. Agents use it to understand.

The old roadmap

Build the meaning infrastructure that lets humans and agents share the same understanding.

For Humans

Turn scattered knowledge into meaning that holds.

Bring anything in — documents, links, meetings, research, raw thought — and move from uncertainty to a defensible answer you can stand behind and share.

For Machines

Agents don't need a pile of documents. They need to know what is true, uncertain, supported, contradicted, stale — and what they're allowed to say. Orient gives them that.

For Humans · The product

Five surfaces for turning knowledge into shared understanding.

The visible product. Every action a person takes here quietly produces structured meaning underneath.

01

Capture

Bring anything into Orient — documents, links, notes, voice, meetings, PDFs, raw thoughts. Turn scattered material into structured meaning.

02

Resolve

Ask a question and build an answer that holds — with support, weak spots and open questions made explicit. Move from uncertainty to a defensible answer.

03

Frame

Turn meaning into communication — decks, memos, articles, briefings, narratives. Make the answer land with an audience.

04

Remember

Build personal, team and organisational memory — not storage, meaning memory. Orient remembers what mattered, why, to whom, and how understanding changed.

05

Measure

Before work ships, Orient reads it for clarity, evidence, coherence and audience fit. A Meaning Health Score that says whether the work is ready to move people.

For Machines · The platform

The deeper layer agents consume.

Agents inherit the organisation's understanding — its evidence, its constraints, its current answers — instead of guessing from raw retrieval.

01

Context

Give agents the right working memory — the question, the current answer, the relevant claims, weak spots and constraints. Meaning they can use, not chunks to sift.

02

Grounding

Let agents act from evidence, not hallucination. What supports this? What contradicts it? Is it stale? Every action tied back to support, uncertainty and provenance.

03

Policy & Constraints

Durable rules of meaning and behaviour — tone, lexical contracts, taboo phrases, accepted risks, decision principles. Agents inherit how the organisation thinks and speaks.

04

Task Framing

Turn a human request into an agent-ready frame — desired outcome, audience, evidence standard, risk level, memory scope. Agents understand the job, not just the prompt.

05

Evaluation

Measure whether an agent preserved meaning — against source, intent, audience and the lexical contract. Checked for meaning integrity, not just factual correctness.
